QIMEN
 _

PIKE is an isogeny-based public-key encryption scheme. Built on top of POKE, PIKE uses pairings to improve performance while keeping the compact keys characteristic of higher-dimensional isogeny constructions.

PRISM is a digital signature scheme derived from an isogeny-based identification protocol. Through higher-dimensional isogenies, PRISM achieves short signatures and competitive signing & verification throughput.

isogeny-based higher-dimensional pairing-improved POKE compact keys
isogeny-based higher-dimensional id-protocol → fiat-shamir short signatures
01 · overview

What is ?

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

QIMEN PIKE is constructed on top of POKE, a recent public-key encryption framework over supersingular isogenies. By introducing pairing-based evaluation in the decryption step, PIKE eliminates a structural bottleneck of the original scheme, yielding faster encryption and decryption without enlarging keys or ciphertexts.

Like its sibling PRISM, PIKE relies on higher-dimensional isogenies — a recent breakthrough that turns the abstract Kani correspondence into a concrete, evaluable primitive.

QIMEN PRISM is obtained from an identification protocol via the Fiat–Shamir transform. The underlying interactive protocol is sound under standard isogeny assumptions; PRISM compiles it into a non-interactive signature with short challenges and compact responses.

Signing and verification are dominated by evaluations of higher-dimensional isogenies — the same primitive that powers recent SQIsign variants — giving PRISM a favourable trade-off between signature size and signing speed.
